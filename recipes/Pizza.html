Pizza

Description

Pizza[a][1] is an Italian, specifically Neapolitan, dish typically consisting of a flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomato, cheese, and other ingredients, baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

Ingredients

  • Dough Ingredients:
  • Flour (all-purpose or bread flour)
  • Yeast (active dry or instant)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Olive oil
  • Sugar (optional, for yeast activation)
  • Sauce Ingredients:
  • Tomato sauce (smooth or pureed)
  • Herbs (such as oregano, basil, garlic powder)
  • Spices (such as garlic powder, onion powder)
  • Optional: Onion and garlic

Steps:

  1. Start with a crust. A wide range of pizza crust mixes, pre-made crusts and refrigerated doughs are available. Try a whole-grain crust to get the benefits of fiber and a range of vitamins and minerals. Or try making mini pizzas with pita bread, English muffin halves, bagel halves, lavash or naan as the base. Gluten-free and vegetarian crust options are available, too.
  2. Add a sauce. Go light on the sauce to avoid a soggy crust. You can use the old standby tomato-based pizza sauce, or try:
    • Alfredo sauce
    • Pesto (made of basil, olive oil and other ingredients)
    • Asian peanut sauce
    • Barbecue sauce
    • Hummus (seasoned chickpea dip)
    • Salsa
  3. Add some veggies, such as:
    • Artichoke hearts, sliced
    • Red, yellow and green peppers, sliced or diced
    • Broccoli florets
    • Carrots, shredded
    • Spinach leaves
    • Mushrooms, sliced
    • Onion, diced
    • Tomatoes, diced
  4. Try some fruit on your pizza, such as:
    • Apples, thinly sliced
    • Pears, thinly sliced
    • Mango, thinly sliced
    • Pineapple chunks
  5. Add some protein, such as:
    • Bacon, cooked, drained and crumbled
    • Ground beef, cooked and drained
    • Black or white beans
    • Ham, cubed or sliced
    • Chicken, grilled
    • Italian sausage, cooked and drained
    • Crab meat, cooked and drained
    • Egg, hard-cooked and chopped
    • Pork, shredded
    • Shrimp
  6. Add cheese. Mozzarella is the typical pizza topper, but try:
    • Cheddar
    • Havarti
    • Swiss
    • Feta
    • Parmesan
  7. Bake your creation in a hot oven (450°F or above). Adjust baking time based on toppings. You can also grill it for a smoky flavor.

Whole-Wheat Pizza Crust

  • ¾ c. whole-wheat flour
  • ¾ c. all-purpose flour
  • 1 package quick-rising yeast (2¼ tsp.)
  • ¾ tsp. salt
  • ¼ tsp. sugar
  • ½ c. warm water (120 to 130°F)
  • 2 tsp. extra-virgin olive oil

Combine flours, yeast, salt, and sugar in a bowl. Mix water and oil separately, then combine until dough forms. Adjust with water/flour if too dry/sticky. Knead, cover, and let rest 10–20 minutes. Preheat oven to 500°F, roll out, brush with olive oil, and poke with fork. Makes 10 servings. Each: 80 cal, 1.5g fat, 3g protein, 14g carbs, 1g fiber, 180mg sodium.

Pizza Crust

  • 2¼ c. all-purpose flour
  • 1 Tbsp. baking powder
  • ¼ tsp. salt
  • 1 c. skim milk
  • ¼ c. unsalted butter (room temp)

Preheat to 450°F. Mix all, form dough, roll out. Top and bake 10–12 minutes. Makes 10 servings. Each: 150 cal, 5g fat, 4g protein, 23g carbs, 0g fiber, 220mg sodium.

Homemade Pizza Sauce

  • 1 15-ounce can tomato sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 Tbsp. minced fresh onion
  • 1 tsp. dried parsley
  • 1½ tsp. dried oregano
  • 1 Tbsp. grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ tsp. dried basil

Combine all in pot. Cook over medium heat 20 min. Makes 16 servings. Each: 10 cal, 0g fat, 0g protein, 2g carbs, 1g fiber, 130mg sodium.
